ABOUT ME

The Big Bang Therapy is a game that started as a game design school project, but that I decided to keep developing it with friends.

 

In this game, you play as a mentally ill person in an asylum, that has to act like he/she isn't ill anymore.

The game is divided in 2 parts: The psychotherapy and the night. During the psychotherapy, you have to imagine a 1-minute speech in which you have to place the 3 words associated with your illness. Everything is allowed, as long as you can hear the words in the speech.

During the night, you're gonna hide things in your "friends" cell. If you think you know what illness someone has, you can try to make him lose credibility.

 

The winner is the one that has the most credibility after 3 turns.

What I then worked on was to find artists to work with and add drinking / cheating rules.

The art you can see was made by Maxime Ferlin.

 

It is currently on pause, but I'll try and bring it back to life ASAP !

Marxs is a game made during Alakajam 2. Our goal was to propose an aesthetic experience and my task was to create an infinite runner, which is why the LD and feedback are so bad since it was my first time doing a procedural game.

 

The theme was "You can't see everything" so we decided to make a runner in which there are obstacles in two worlds, and you have to travel between them to avoid every obstacle. Both worlds have their own graphics and music and we tried to make the transition as smooth as possible.

 

It was a great experience because I learned a lot about code, and I loved the universe in which the game takes place and we all had lots of problems but we learned new tools & ways to create a game !

 

I really liked this game and would like to redo it now that I know more about Unity and procedural generation of games.

Rubicatpocalypse is a game we made during the Global Game Jam 40 (link)

 

The theme was "transmission" so we had the idea to mix a treasure hunt and a zombie game.

 

You play as a young Game Designer that has to survive zombie waves. The trick is, every ammo and health container is locked by a code. To unlock them, you must contact a friend and he has to find the codes hidden in the Serre Numérique (our school). The level design of the game is also the Serre Numérique, so you almost know where the codes are hidden.

 

Unfortunatly, you can only beat the game at the Serre Numérique, since we had to hide the codes in real life manually.
