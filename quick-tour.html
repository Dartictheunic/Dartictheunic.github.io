The journey

I first did a Game Design bachelor before entering the world of design. A workshop with Renault made me realise Game Design and UX had a lot of similarities (understanding the human brain, lots of testing, iterative processes) and that my Game Designer background could even be an advantage. I knew how it felt to see players not understand something that seems obvious to you, knew the benefits of rewards in a learning process...

 

This is why I joined Rubika's ISD - Interaction in order to get more knowledge about the design process and skills, and UX methodologies.

Methodology

As an UX designer, the answer is obvious: putting myself in the shoes and head of users in order to understand and satisfy their needs. My favorite combination is mindmapping with user interviews. I feel like that's when the solutions are almost given by the users themselves.

 

When it comes to managing, I've had the chance of working with a lot of awesome personalities. I feel like the best ideas and project come from very different people expressing themselves and trying to find a way to move forward each in their own way.

 

A teammate once told me "Alexis, you're not an addition to the team you're a multiplicator". I'm not putting in here to brag, but because I think it really describes the way I work in a team: trying to help everybody to express themselves and work without having to worry.

Communication

In each and every project I work on, I try to create either a Process or a Trailer video.

 

I think it's very important to communicate on what you're doing, especially in the UX field. One image is worth a thousand words, and a short video will create empathy for your project, as well as informing more effectively than with just a speech.
