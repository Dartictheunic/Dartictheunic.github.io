Karp & Dem's is our first mobile game, made during the first semester of our third year.

 

You play as two fishes : Karp and Dem's and you're trying to get back to the ocean.

When you tap either left or right the corresponding fish jumps and you can tilt your phone to make them move and right.

 

Our goal was to design a game based on music but mobile friendly. You can play the game without sound but the level design was made based on the musics to make you feel like you're playing the music yourself.

 

My work was be to make sure the project followed our deadlines, programming the controller (mobile inputs, accelerometer, complex scoring system), game design, level design and assets integration.

La Selva is our second year project: a Zelda like made in pixel art. We chose to use a post-Renaissance uchronia

 

La selva is a carboniferous-like jungle that grew because of the spores Christophe Colomb brought from the new world. Due to its proliferation, most people in Europe died trying to resist and the few survivors live in walled-up cities.

 

In this game, you play as Amalia, a 16 years old girl whose father is missing and who tries to learn what happened to him...

 

My work was be to make sure the project followed our deadlines, programming mechanics in Unity (fight system, lights, IA), & game design. I also made the trailer you can see up there (the music was made by Hadrien Auvray)

Time Trouble is our First Year project, it's a board game about time traveling. You play a team of adventurers teleported in an out of time zone and must escape before you're trapped forever.

 

My tasks were of course the game design of the project, but also it's producing. We had to print everything (the cards, the box and the tiles) and I was the one in charge of it. Due to low budget, I had to find the best price for every part of the game, which was challenging but also a great experience.

 

In terms of game design, our goal was to design a game that used height in order to picture the time passing by. When playing, you put tiles representing different eras on the table, but you can put tiles on top of other ones, combined with the possibility to move them we felt like it was the best way to picture an unstable space-time dimention.
