In this game your goal is simple: grab as much marbles as possible !

 

When the game starts, someone has to take the Dumper™ and release all the marbles it contains on the board. Each player then grabs his or her Controller™ and starts running around the table to collect the marbles. The Board™ where the marbles bounce can tilt, making it possible to change the course of the marbles !

 

Created by: Nicolas Bekhazi, Valentin Bourgogne, Lila Grimaldi, Martin Jacob, Alexis Jouanneau.

Special thanks to : Eddy Sarot & Tatiana Vilela dos Santos

The Bwiper is a object designed in duo in 6 days, the goal was to analyze what was going wrong in airports (we took Paris' most famous one) from a user point-of-view. My teammate and me realized what was one of the worst part of the airport experience was between security checks and the moment you sit in the airplane. It's all about waiting without information and people lining up for minutes to hours at a slow pace.

 

 

The Bwiper is a necklace that you swap against your ticket when entering the waiting area. When picking up your Bwiper you can give your cabin luggage to the hostess so it's put in the airplane before boarding.

 

 

When boarding, instead of everyone being called at the same time and queuing up for nothing, your Bwiper will vibrate and emit light corresponding to your boarding door's color. People are called in smaller groups and hand over their Bwipers where their tickets are stored. This allows for faster and tinier queues, thus making user spend less time waiting on their feet.

 

It was quite challenging to go through the whole process of designing over 6 days including analysis, 5 creative phases and creating plans & 3D-renders but it was really interesting.
