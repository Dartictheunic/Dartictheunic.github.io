La Selva is our second year project: a Zelda like made in pixel art. We chose to use a post-Renaissance uchronia

 

La selva is a carboniferous-like jungle that grew because of the spores Christophe Colomb brought from the new world. Due to its proliferation, most people in Europe died trying to resist and the few survivors live in walled-up cities.

 

In this game, you play as Amalia, a 16 years old girl whose father is missing and who tries to learn what happened to him...

 

My work was to make sure the project followed our deadlines, programming mechanics in Unity (fight system, lights, IA), & game design. I also made the trailer you can see up there (the music was made by Hadrien Auvray)

Karp & Dem's is our first mobile game, made during the first semester of our third year.

 

You play as two fishes : Karp and Dem's and you're trying to get back to the ocean.

When you tap either left or right the corresponding fish jumps and you can tilt your phone to make them move and right.

 

Our goal was to design a game based on music but mobile friendly. You can play the game without sound but the level design was made based on the musics to make you feel like you're playing the music yourself.

 

My work was to make sure the project followed our deadlines, programming the controller (mobile inputs, accelerometer, complex scoring system), game design, level design and assets integration.

Project Haven is a VR game in which you are on a leaf-boat and have to make it move forward. This game was made for General Electrics as they asked us to use VR to relief breast biopsy patients.

To make the boat move, you have to breath slowly, in harmony with your companion. This makes the patient feel less stressed and it aims to put her in a meditative state. As the patient breathes, she makes choices in the environment, changing the weather or the landscape.

The headset itself is non intrusive, not attached to the head so the operation can continue and the patient can choose what she wants to see (or not).

 

It was a great project since we were not between game students so we had a lot of new problematics as well as different personalities. Designing a VR experience for hospitals is something I really enjoyed, as it shows games can become a solution to anxiety and stress in a medical environment which is something that is really important to me.

 

I was in charge of leading the project and the design of all the aspects of the experience (headset, game, seat...) and had to manage game artists, vehicle designers and 3D animators. I loved doing it as I was out of my comfort zone  but everything went well and we all enjoyed working together.

Time Trouble is our First Year project, it's a board game about time traveling. You play a team of adventurers teleported in an out of time zone and must escape before you're trapped forever.

 

My tasks were of course the game design of the project, but also it's producing. We had to print everything (the cards, the box and the tiles) and I was the one in charge of it. Due to low budget, I had to find the best price for every part of the game, which was challenging but also a great experience.

 

In terms of game design, our goal was to design a game that used height in order to picture the time passing by. When playing, you put tiles representing different eras on the table, but you can put tiles on top of other ones, combined with the possibility to move them we felt like it was the best way to picture an unstable space-time dimension.
